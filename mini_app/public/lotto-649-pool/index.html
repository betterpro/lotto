Lotto 6/49 pool guide

A Lotto 6/49 pool lets a group organize participation for a familiar Canadian draw. The key is not complexity; it is clarity around shares, tickets and prize handling.

Choose a simple share price

A simple share price makes the round easy to understand. Members should know how many shares they bought, how many total shares exist, and what percentage of the round they own.

Keep each draw separate

Lottery pools become confusing when tickets for several draws are mixed together. A separate round for each draw keeps ticket proof and share ownership clean.

Post ticket photos before the draw

Members should see the Lotto 6/49 ticket before the result is known. Good ticket photos reduce disputes and make it easier for members to verify the group played the numbers it says it played.

Decide how to handle free plays

Some prizes may include free plays or small returns. The group should know ahead of time whether those roll into the next round, are credited by cash value, or are handled another written way.

LottoChee gives each round a written record so members can see share ownership and ticket proof in one place.

Lottery play involves chance. Do not treat a Lotto 6/49 pool as an investment or income plan.
